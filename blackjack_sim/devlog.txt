Entry #5

Decided on a project scope and novel contribution

Framework: a configurable Monte Carlo simulator where rule set, counting
system, betting policy, player error rate, and bankroll are pluggable.

Three counting systems compared: Hi-Lo, KO, Zen.

Three focus points:
1. Standard comparison - EV, variance, win rate under perfect play,
   infinite bankroll.
2. Robustness to error - same three systems with random count perturbation
    at different error rates. EV degradation under realistic imperfect play.
3. Risk of ruin - same three systems, finite starting bankrolls, fixed
   bet units. Probability of going broke within different number of hands.

Differentiator: most studies report EV under perfect play and infinite bankroll.
This project additionally quantifies degradation under realistic error and survival
under finite bankroll, producing a more complete picture relevant to real play.
