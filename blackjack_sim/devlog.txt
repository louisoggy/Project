Entry #5
Decided on the project scope and novel contribution.

Building a configurable Monte Carlo simulator where the rule set, counting
system, betting policy, player error rate and bankroll are all pluggable.
Comparing three counting systems: Hi-Lo, KO, Zen.

Three focus points:
1. Standard comparison, EV, variance and win rate under perfect play and
   infinite bankroll.
2. Robustness to error, same three systems with random count perturbation
   at different error rates, measuring EV degradation under imperfect play.
3. Risk of ruin, same three systems with finite bankrolls and fixed bet
   units, measuring probability of going broke over a number of hands.

Majority of similar projects / studies only report EV under perfect play and infinite bankroll.
Will also look at degradation under error and survival under bankroll, closer to real play.

Entry #6

Expanded basic_strategy() to: hit, stand, double, or split.

Signature changed to basic_strategy(player_hand, dealer_upcard,
can_double=True, can_split=True). The can_double and can_split flags
let callers disable actions that aren't legal in context (e.g.
can_double=False after a hit, can_split=False on a non-pair or after
the resplit limit is reached).

Structured in three layers, evaluated in priority order:
pairs, soft totals, hard totals. Matches standard published
basic strategy for the locked rule set (S17, DAS, double-on-any-two,
3:2 blackjack).

Wrote test_basic.py with 52 cases: all 10 pair rules with
representative dealer upcards, all soft totals from soft 13 through
soft 19, hard totals across all key decision boundaries (hard 9-12,
13-16, 17), and edge cases involving the can_double / can_split flags.

Initial test run: 51/52 passed. One failure on soft 18 vs dealer 3
with can_double=False. Function fell back to hit instead of stand.
Cause: the can_double=False branch for soft 18 only mapped to stand
for upcards 2, 7, 8. Upcards 3-6 incorrectly fell through to hit.
This bug would have mattered after splits, where doubling becomes
unavailable on the new hand and soft 18 vs 3-6 is a recurring case.
Fixed so the can_double=False fallback for soft 18 vs 3-6 returns
stand. After fix: 52/52 passed.

play_hand still uses the two argument form,
updating play_hand is the next step.

Entry #10
Wired the Counter into a new simulation function run_counter,
added deck penetration.

run_counter creates one Shoe and one Counter,
records the true count before each hand (what the player would bet on),
plays the hand, then has the Counter observe every card dealt that hand.
Dealer hand observed once, each split hand's player cards observed.
Counter resets on reshuffle.
Returns the outcome counts plus the list of true counts seen.

Penetration: added a penetration param to Shoe (default 0.75) and a needs_reshuffle() method.
Reshuffle now triggers when 75% of the shoe has been dealt,
instead of dealing down to the last 20 cards.
run_simulation and run_counter both use needs_reshuffle() now.

check via count_distribution.py: mean true count ~0, curve centred on 0,
outcomes unchanged at 39/48/8.5/4.4 so gameplay wasn't affected.

Entry #11
Added bet spreading tied to the true count,
this is the step where counting starts producing an actual profit.

New bet_ramp(true_count) function returns the base bet in units:
1 unit at true count 1 or below, 2 at TC 2, 4 at TC 3, 6 at TC 4, 8 at TC 5+ (capped).
Floors the true count for the lookup, negatives bet the minimum.
run_counter takes a betting_policy param defaulting to bet_ramp so the ramp
can be swapped later.

run_counter now computes a base bet before each hand,
tracks net profit and total wagered (scaling by outcome bet for doubles and splits,
blackjack paid 3:2 on the base bet), and returns total_net, total_wagered,
ev_per_hand and edge_pct alongside the old keys.

test_bet.py over 1,000,000 hands, runs twice: edge came out +0.32,
so a small but real and stable player advantage.
Average bet 1.63 units. The edge is positive,
counting plus bet spread beats the house.
on the low side which fits the conservative ramp and 75% penetration,
both could be pushed harder for more edge at the cost of more variance,
what the risk of ruin experiment will look at.

bet_ramp spot checked across true counts

Entry #15
Wired the per-system bet ramps into run_counter and got all three systems
comparing fairly.

run_counter now branches on whether the system is balanced.
Hi-Lo and Zen bet off the true count through bet_ramp like before.
KO bets off its raw running count through ko_bet_ramp,
no division by decks remaining, with the ramp anchored to KO's key count (-4)
and pivot (+4).

Before calibration KO was a mess: feeding its running count into the
true count ramp made it bet near max almost every hand (avg bet 6.64) and it
came out at -0.28% edge. After giving KO its own ramp the avg bet dropped to
1.74, in line with the other two, and the edge went positive.

Ranking matches theory: all three beat the house, Zen best (multi-level,
highest correlation), the simpler systems trail. Single 500k runs so the
exact numbers have noise, hi_lo and ko are close enough to swap on another
run, but the shape is right.

All three core counting systems now implemented.

Entry #16
Added a player error parameter to run_counter.
With probability error_rate the player misreads the count by +/-1 on a given hand,
applied to a local copy of the count used for the bet decision only.
The real running count is never touched, so the error is a transient misread
not a permanent corruption.
error_rate 0 reproduces the perfect play results exactly.
Only bet sizing is affected, basic strategy play is unchanged.

Then started a new file experiments.py for experiment tooling, keeping it
separate from the engine. First thing in it is run_trials, a harness that
repeats a run_counter config several times and returns the mean edge plus
the standard error, along with the raw per run edges.

Why: bet spread edges are really noisy,
needs to be a mean over trials with an error bar

Entry #17
Added run_ruin for the risk of ruin experiment.
Plays against a finite bankroll (measured in units) until either the bankroll hits 0,
below, or a max hand limit is reached, and reports which happened.

Ruin is defined as bankroll reaching 0 or below.
While the bankroll is positive the player keeps going, and if it's positive but smaller than the
bet the ramp wants, they bet whatever they have left rather than stopping.
Uses the same count, bet ramp and error logic as run_counter so the two stay consistent.
Returns broke (bool), hands_played, final_bankroll and the config.
Broke rate falls as bankroll rises, expected.
